About

In Rwanda, a passionate group of future public health professionals is not waiting for tomorrow to make a difference. The University of Rwanda Public Health Students' Association (URPHSA), founded in 2017, is actively addressing community health challenges, bridging the gap between academic knowledge and real-world impact, and contributing to the achievement of key Sustainable Development Goals.


URPHSA's aim is clear-to contribute meaningfully to the achievement of critical Sustainable Development Goals in Rwanda, encompassing poverty reduction, food security, health and well-being, clean water and sanitation, climate action, and environmental sustainability. Their mission is to nurture the next generation of public health professionals by equipping them with essential leadership, management, communication, and research skills, empowering them to tackle community health challenges effectively. URPHSA envisions itself as a leading student association that empowers the Rwandan public health workforce, fostering skilled and passionate professionals dedicated to equitably solving community health problems and creating a healthier, more equitable society.
