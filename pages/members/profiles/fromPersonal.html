About

25-year-old Cynthia Umutoni is turning her personal story of loss into powerful advocacy. Cynthia’s journey began with the discovery in her childhood that she herself was an orphan — raised by her grandmother after losing both parents as an infant. That realization reshaped her dream: not just to help orphans, but to become a voice for those who feel unseen, unloved, or forgotten. “I used to think I would become rich and raise orphans,” she shares, “but learning I was one of them gave that dream a deeper meaning.”


Her work includes community outreach, public speaking, and school-based education, ensuring that adolescents have access to life skills, counseling, and safe spaces to speak up. Through her efforts, she’s empowered girls to leave sex work, educated youth on HIV prevention, and inspired young women to pursue new futures.
