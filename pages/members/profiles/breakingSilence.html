About

For 26-year-old Vidha Kabera, advocacy began with a simple but painful realization — too many of her peers were silently suffering. “I saw friends struggling with mental health issues, but they had nowhere to turn. Society prioritizes physical health, but mental well-being is often ignored or even ridiculed”, she says. That silence pushed her to act.

In a country where stigma still surrounds mental illness, Vidha works to normalize conversations, especially among the younger generation. “Being someone’s ‘safe space’ doesn’t need a building,” she says. “It just takes presence and empathy.” Her sessions have empowered young people to speak openly, seek help, and feel understood for the first time.
